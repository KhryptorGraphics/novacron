## Requirements & Prerequisites

### NovaCron Certified Developer

**Study Requirements:**
- Minimum 100 hours of study
- Complete "DWCP Fundamentals" course
- Complete 5+ hands-on labs
- Pass practice exams (80%+ average)

**Prerequisites:**
- Basic programming knowledge (any language)
- Understanding of client-server architecture
- Familiarity with APIs
- No prior DWCP experience required

**Exam Requirements:**
- 60 questions (40 multiple choice, 20 scenario-based)
- 2 practical labs
- 2 hour time limit
- 90% passing score
- Online proctored exam

**Practical Projects:**
- 1 complete project demonstrating DWCP usage
- Submitted via GitHub
- Reviewed by certification team

### NovaCron Certified Architect

**Study Requirements:**
- Minimum 200 hours of study
- Complete "Advanced DWCP Architecture" course
- Complete 10+ hands-on labs
- Pass practice exams (85%+ average)

**Prerequisites:**
- NovaCron Certified Developer (required)
- 2+ years professional development experience
- 500+ community reputation points
- Portfolio of DWCP applications

**Exam Requirements:**
- 80 questions (50 multiple choice, 30 scenario-based)
- 3 practical labs
- 3 hour time limit
- 95% passing score
- Online proctored exam

**Practical Projects:**
- 3 projects showcasing architecture skills
- At least 1 multi-region deployment
- Performance optimization demonstrated
- Security implementation

### NovaCron Certified Expert

**Study Requirements:**
- Minimum 500 hours of advanced study
- Complete all advanced courses
- Mentor 5+ developers
- Contribute to community (articles, talks, open source)

**Prerequisites:**
- NovaCron Certified Architect (required)
- 5+ years distributed systems experience
- 2,000+ community reputation points

**Exam Requirements:**
- 100 questions (60 multiple choice, 40 case studies)
- 5 practical labs
- 4 hour time limit
- 95% passing score
- In-person or online proctored exam

**Practical Projects:**
- 5 complex projects

- Community impact

## Exam Format

### Question Types

**1. Multiple Choice (40-60%)**
- Single correct answer
- 4-5 options per question
- Tests knowledge recall
- 2-3 minutes per question

*Example:*
```
Which DWCP method is used to establish initial connection?

A) dwcp.connect()
B) dwcp.initialize()
C) dwcp.start()
D) dwcp.establish()
E) dwcp.join()

Answer: A
```

**2. Multiple Answer (10-15%)**
- Multiple correct answers
- Select all that apply
- Tests comprehensive understanding
- 3-4 minutes per question

*Example:*
```
Which of the following are valid DWCP consistency levels?
(Select all that apply)

‚ñ° EVENTUAL
‚ñ° STRONG
‚ñ° WEAK
‚ñ° STRICT
‚ñ° CAUSAL

Answers: EVENTUAL, STRONG, CAUSAL
```

**3. Scenario-Based (20-30%)**
- Real-world situations
- Apply knowledge to solve problems
- Code analysis
- 4-5 minutes per question

*Example:*
```
You have a distributed application experiencing consistency issues
during network partitions. Which approach should you use?

[Scenario details provided]

A) Implement eventual consistency with conflict resolution
B) Use strict serialization with blocking
C) Deploy active-passive failover
D) Increase timeout values

Answer: A (with explanation required)
```

**4. Case Studies (Expert level)**
- Complex multi-part scenarios
- System design challenges
- Architecture decisions
- 15-20 minutes per case

**5. Practical Labs (20-30%)**
- Hands-on coding exercises
- Real sandbox environment
- Automated validation
- 20-30 minutes per lab

*Example Lab:*
```
Build a multi-node coordinator that handles:
- Leader election
- State synchronization
- Partition tolerance
- Performance under 100ms latency

Validation tests provided
Sandbox environment: Go 1.21, 4GB RAM
```

### Exam Structure

- Multiple choice: 1 point each

- Developer: 90% (54/60 points)

**Partial Credit:**

## Exam Day Procedures

### Before the Exam

**30 Minutes Before:**
- Close all applications except browser
- Clear your desk (only allowed items)
- Position webcam to show your face and workspace
- Ensure good lighting
- Test audio/video one final time

**Allowed Items:**
- Valid government-issued ID
- Water (in clear container)
- Blank paper (2 sheets) and pencil
- Calculator (if applicable)

**NOT Allowed:**
- Books, notes, study materials
- Mobile phones
- Smart watches
- Additional monitors
- Headphones
- Other people in room

### Check-In Process

**Identity Verification:**
1. Proctor will join your session
2. Show ID to camera (all sides)
3. Show your workspace (360¬∞ pan)
4. Show blank scratch paper
5. Read and accept exam policies

**Environment Scan:**
- Pan camera around room
- Show under desk
- Show monitor/screen
- Verify no unauthorized materials

### During the Exam

**Proctoring:**
- Continuous video monitoring
- Screen recording
- AI-powered anomaly detection
- Human proctor oversight

**Rules:**
- Stay in camera view at all times
- No talking (except to proctor)
- No looking away from screen excessively
- No unauthorized breaks
- Follow all proctor instructions

**Navigation:**
- Answer questions in any order
- Flag questions for review
- Time remaining displayed
- Submit when complete or time expires

**Breaks:**
- No scheduled breaks for 2-hour exams
- One 10-minute break for 3-hour exams
- Two 10-minute breaks for 4-hour exams
- Time continues during breaks
- Re-verification required after break

### Suspicious Activity

**Flagged Behaviors:**
- Looking away frequently
- Talking or moving lips
- Leaving camera view
- Unauthorized materials
- Additional person detected
- Unusual device activity

**Consequences:**
- Warning from proctor (first instance)
- Exam termination (repeated violations)
- Exam invalidation
- Possible ban from future exams

### Technical Issues

**If You Experience Issues:**
1. Use chat to contact proctor immediately
2. Explain the issue clearly
3. Follow proctor instructions
4. Issues are logged and reviewed
5. Extra time may be granted for platform issues

**Common Issues:**
- Internet disconnection: Reconnect within 5 minutes
- Browser crash: Restart, rejoin session
- Proctoring software issue: Contact support
- Hardware failure: Reschedule without penalty

## Renewal Requirements

Certifications expire 2 years after issuance. Renewal maintains your certified status.

### Continuing Education Units (CEUs)

**Required CEUs by Level:**
- Developer: 20 CEUs
- Architect: 30 CEUs
- Expert: 50 CEUs

**How to Earn CEUs:**

| Activity | CEUs |
|----------|------|
| Complete advanced course (10hrs) | 5 CEUs |
| Attend conference/workshop | 3-10 CEUs |
| Speak at conference | 10 CEUs |
| Publish technical article | 5 CEUs |
| Contribute to open source | 5-15 CEUs |
| Mentor other developers | 5 CEUs/mentee |
| Pass recertification exam | 30 CEUs |

### Renewal Process

**Option 1: CEU-Based Renewal**
1. Accumulate required CEUs (tracked automatically)
2. Submit renewal application (3 months before expiry)
3. Pay renewal fee ($99-$199)
4. Certificate renewed for 2 years

**Option 2: Exam-Based Renewal**
1. Take recertification exam (same level)
2. Pass with 85% score (slightly easier than original)
3. Pay exam fee (50% of original)
4. Certificate renewed for 2 years

**Option 3: Upgrade**
1. Pursue higher level certification
2. Pass higher level exam
3. Previous certification auto-renewed
4. Both certificates active

### Expired Certification

**Grace Period:**
- 6-month grace period after expiry
- Can still renew using CEUs
- After grace period: Must retake full exam

**Lapsed Status:**
- Certificate marked as expired
- Not valid for employment verification
- Removed from public directory
- Can be reinstated by retaking exam

## FAQs

### General

**Q: How long is certification valid?**
A: 2 years from issue date. Renewal required.

**Q: Can I take exams in different languages?**
A: Currently available in English, Spanish, Mandarin, and Japanese. More languages coming soon.

**Q: Are accommodations available for disabilities?**
A: Yes! Contact certification@novacron.io at least 2 weeks before exam to arrange accommodations.

**Q: Can I upgrade from one level to the next?**
A: Yes! Complete prerequisites and register for the higher level exam.

### Exam

**Q: Can I use documentation during the exam?**
A: No. Exams are closed-book. All information must be from memory.

**Q: What happens if I lose internet connection?**
A: You have 5 minutes to reconnect. Time continues, but issues are logged. May receive extra time if platform issue.

**Q: Can I take a break during the exam?**
A: Breaks are allowed for 3+ hour exams, but time continues during break. Re-verification required.

**Q: How is the exam scored?**
A: Automated scoring for multiple choice. Labs are auto-validated. Human review for any flags or appeals.

### Results

**Q: When will I get my results?**
A: Within 5 business days. Immediate preliminary results, official after proctor review.

**Q: Can I see which questions I got wrong?**
A: You receive a score breakdown by topic but not specific question answers.

**Q: What is the pass rate?**
A: Overall pass rate is ~75%. Higher for those who complete study plans.

**Q: What if I don't agree with my score?**
A: You can request a review within 7 days. Fee refunded if scoring error found.

### Certification

**Q: Is the certification recognized by employers?**
A: Yes! 50+ companies recognize NovaCron certifications, with more added regularly.

**Q: Can I put the certification on my LinkedIn?**
A: Absolutely! Use the digital badge and link to verification page.

**Q: Does certification expire?**
A: Yes, after 2 years. Renewal required through CEUs or re-examination.

**Q: Can certification be revoked?**
A: Yes, for cheating, policy violations, or unethical behavior. Appeals process available.

### Exam Tips

**1. Time Management**
- Note time limits
- Don't get stuck on one question
- Flag and return to difficult questions
- Leave time for review

**2. Read Carefully**
- Read full question
- Note keywords (always, never, best, etc.)
- Eliminate wrong answers
- Choose best answer, not just correct

**3. Use Scratch Paper**
- Draw diagrams
- Work through scenarios
- Track flagged questions
- Plan lab approaches

**4. Lab Strategy**
- Read requirements fully
- Plan before coding
- Test incrementally
- Submit working solution even if incomplete

**5. Stay Calm**
- Deep breaths if stressed
- Skip and return to hard questions
- Trust your preparation
- Don't second-guess excessively
