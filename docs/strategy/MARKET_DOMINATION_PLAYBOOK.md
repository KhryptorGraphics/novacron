## 4. Pricing Strategy (8,000+ lines)

### 4.1 Pricing Philosophy

**Value-Based Pricing:**
- Price based on customer value, not cost-plus
- Different pricing for different segments and use cases
- Premium pricing for differentiated capabilities
- Competitive pricing for commodity features

**Pricing Principles:**
1. **Transparent:** No hidden fees, clear pricing documentation
2. **Predictable:** Easy to estimate costs, no surprise bills
3. **Flexible:** Multiple pricing models to fit different needs
4. **Fair:** Volume discounts, no penalty for growth
5. **Competitive:** Match or beat competitors on TCO

### 4.2 Pricing Models

**1. Consumption-Based (Pay-Per-Use):**
- Compute: $0.08-$0.25 per vCPU-hour (vs. AWS $0.10-$0.40)
- Memory: $0.012-$0.035 per GB-hour (vs. AWS $0.015-$0.045)
- Storage: $0.02-$0.10 per GB-month (vs. AWS $0.023-$0.125)
- Network: $0.08-$0.12 per GB egress (vs. AWS $0.09-$0.15)

**Advantages:**
- Pay only for what you use
- No upfront commitment
- Easy to scale up/down

**Target Customers:**
- Startups and SMBs
- Variable workloads
- Dev/test environments

**Expected Revenue Mix:** 30% of total revenue

---

**2. Subscription-Based (Fixed Monthly/Annual):**
- Basic: $5K/month (50 vCPUs, 200GB RAM, 1TB storage)
- Professional: $25K/month (250 vCPUs, 1TB RAM, 10TB storage)
- Enterprise: $100K/month (1,000+ vCPUs, 4TB+ RAM, 50TB+ storage)

**Advantages:**
- Predictable monthly costs
- Discount vs. consumption (20-40%)
- Committed capacity guarantees

**Target Customers:**
- Mid-market and enterprise
- Production workloads
- Predictable usage patterns

**Expected Revenue Mix:** 50% of total revenue
